The Math
Accumulation phase

Your balance at retirement is the future value of your current savings plus the future value of your monthly contributions (an ordinary annuity):

$$B_R = B_0 \cdot (1+r)^n \;+\; PMT \cdot \frac{(1+r)^n - 1}{r}$$
$B_0$current net worth
$r = R/12$monthly return rate
$n$months until retirement
$PMT$monthly contribution
$B_R$nest egg at retirement
Drawdown phase

Each month your balance earns interest then pays expenses. The recurrence $B_{t+1} = B_t(1+r) - E$ solves to:

$$B_t = B_R \cdot (1+r)^t \;-\; E \cdot \frac{(1+r)^t - 1}{r}$$

Setting $B_t = 0$ and solving for $t$ gives months until depletion:

$$t = -\frac{\ln\!\left(1 - \dfrac{B_R \cdot r}{E}\right)}{\ln(1+r)}$$

Valid when $E > B_R \cdot r$ — i.e. expenses exceed monthly portfolio earnings. Otherwise the portfolio never depletes.
